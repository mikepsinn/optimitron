# Pilot Spec: US Federal Optimocracy Scorecard

## Objective
Demonstrate a reproducible, evidence‑based advisory layer that produces policy and budget recommendations for the US federal government.

## Duration
12 months

## Deliverables
1. Monthly US federal policy ranking report.
2. Monthly US federal budget optimization report.
3. Verification layer with at least three independent sources per metric.
4. Public audit artifacts: inputs, methodology, and outputs.

## Scope
- Budget categories: federal major categories (defense, health, education, infrastructure, etc.).
- Policies: cross‑jurisdiction evidence on high‑impact reforms.
- Metrics: median real after‑tax income and healthy life years.

## Success Criteria
1. Reports generated on schedule with reproducible inputs.
2. Verification agreement scores above threshold (e.g., CV < 15%).
3. At least one external partner publishes or uses the outputs.

## Risks
1. Data availability and consistency across sources.
2. Perceived bias or capture concerns.
3. Misinterpretation of advisory recommendations.

## Mitigations
1. Multi‑source verification and transparent provenance.
2. Open methodology and external review.
3. Clear framing: advisory, not authoritative.
